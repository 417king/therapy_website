My Approach

How I Work

01

We Start With You

The first sessions are about getting to know you — your history, your patterns, what you're hoping changes. No agenda to rush through. We move at your pace.

02

We Work Together

Therapy is a collaboration. I'll bring curiosity, honesty, and evidence-based tools. You'll bring your experience and your willingness. Neither of us has all the answers — and that's okay.

03

We Track What's Shifting

Change is rarely dramatic. We'll regularly check in on how things are feeling — in sessions and in your life — so therapy stays meaningful and not just habitual.

What I Offer

Services

Individual Therapy

One-on-one sessions for adults working through anxiety, depression, trauma, life transitions, identity, and more. Sessions are 50 minutes, held weekly or biweekly.

Anxiety & Stress

Practical, grounded support for people who feel overwhelmed, chronically stressed, or stuck in cycles of worry. We'll work on understanding it and changing your relationship with it.

Life Transitions

Career shifts, relationship changes, loss, moves, new roles — major changes can shake your sense of self. Therapy can help you navigate them with more clarity and less fear.

Relationship Patterns

Whether it's how you show up in romantic relationships, friendships, or family — we can explore the patterns that keep repeating and figure out where they come from.

Your Rights

Good Faith Estimate

You have the right to receive a "Good Faith Estimate" explaining how much your medical care will cost. Under the law, health care providers need to give patients who don't have insurance or who are not using insurance an estimate of the bill for medical items and services.

You have the right to receive a Good Faith Estimate for the total expected cost of any non-emergency items or services. Make sure your provider gives you one in writing at least 1 business day before your service. You can also ask your provider for a Good Faith Estimate before you schedule anything.

If you receive a bill that is at least $400 more than your Good Faith Estimate, you can dispute the bill. For questions or more information, visit cms.gov/nosurprises.

My Story

Hi, I'm Maya Calloway.

I'm a Licensed Marriage and Family Therapist based in [Location], and I've spent the last [X] years helping adults navigate the moments in life that feel too heavy to carry alone — anxiety, burnout, the kind of grief that doesn't have an obvious name, and relationships that keep hitting the same walls.

I came to therapy through my own experience of it. I know what it's like to sit across from someone and not quite know where to start, to feel like you should have it more together by now, to wonder if what you're feeling is "bad enough" to deserve help. It is. You don't have to be in crisis to benefit from having a dedicated space to think.

My training is rooted in attachment theory and relational therapy, which means I pay close attention to patterns — how you relate to yourself, how you relate to others, and where those patterns came from. We'll look at the present and the past, but always in service of helping you move forward.

I work with adults on an individual basis, both in person and via telehealth. I'm direct, I'm warm, and I have a sense of humor. Therapy doesn't have to feel heavy the whole time — sometimes we'll laugh. That's okay too.

What I Believe

The values that shape how I work.

01

You are not your diagnosis.

Labels can be useful shorthand, but they're not the whole story. I'm more interested in understanding you as a person than fitting you into a category. We'll use whatever frameworks are helpful and set aside the ones that aren't.

02

The relationship matters.

Research consistently shows that the therapeutic relationship — the trust, safety, and honesty between therapist and client — is one of the strongest predictors of good outcomes. I take that seriously. If something isn't working between us, I want to know.

03

Insight alone isn't enough.

Understanding why you do something is a good start. But real change happens when insight meets practice — when we take what you're learning in sessions and find ways to actually use it in your life. We'll work on both.

04

You deserve care that respects your whole self.

I am committed to practicing in a way that's affirming of all identities — including race, culture, gender, sexuality, religion, and family structure. I'll never ask you to explain or justify who you are.
